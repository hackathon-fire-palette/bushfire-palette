Medical Emergency Integrations

  1. Real-Time Medical Dispatch Coordination: Integrate with ambulance dispatch systems for coordinated fire and medical response. Enable dual-response alerts for incidents involving injury, smoke inhalation, or burns.
  2. Health Risk Mapping: Overlay vulnerable population data (aged care, hospitals, disability services) on bushfire prediction maps. Prioritize evacuation and medical support based on risk zones.
  3. First Responder Medical Profiles: Include paramedic certifications and first aid skills in the roster system. Auto-assign medically trained firefighters to incidents with injury potential.
  4. Integration with Emergency Health Services: Connect with state health databases to access nearest trauma centers, air ambulance availability, and real-time hospital capacity.
  5. Medical Equipment Tracking: Link inventory systems to track medical kits, oxygen tanks, and burn treatment supplies on each fire vehicle. Alert roster managers when equipment is low or expired.
  6. Predictive Health Alerts: Use bushfire simulation data to forecast smoke exposure zones. Trigger alerts for respiratory risk and deploy medical teams proactively.
  7. Incident Reporting and Analytics: Add modules for medical incident logging during bushfire events. Analyze trends in injuries, response times, and treatment outcomes.

Based on AFDRS and the Australian Warning System. Not for operational use.
